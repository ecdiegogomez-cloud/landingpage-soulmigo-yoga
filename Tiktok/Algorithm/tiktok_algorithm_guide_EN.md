# TikTok Algorithm — Complete Guide for Soulmigo

---

## 1. The Core Concept: The For You Page (FYP)

TikTok does not show your content to all your followers. Instead, it shows it to small groups of strangers and measures how they react. If they respond well, the content is shown to larger groups. If not, distribution stops.

This has one major implication:

> You can go viral with 0 followers. Followers matter far less on TikTok than on Instagram.

---

## 2. The Signals the Algorithm Measures

The algorithm scores every video based on the following signals, listed in order of importance:

### Signal 1 — Watch Time / Video Retention

This is the single most important signal.

- What percentage of the video do users watch?
- Do they watch it more than once (loop)?
- A video completed 100% is far more valuable than one abandoned at the 3-second mark.

For Soulmigo: Start with videos of 10 to 15 seconds. Achieving 100% retention is much easier with short videos than with longer ones.

---

### Signal 2 — Shares

The most valuable signal after watch time.

- When someone shares your video, TikTok interprets it as proof that the content has genuine value.
- A share is worth approximately 10 times more than a like.
- Content that gets shared: humour, useful information, content that feels relatable ("this describes me exactly").

For Soulmigo: Videos framed as "if you do yoga and you have this problem..." naturally invite sharing. People send them to friends.

---

### Signal 3 — Comments

Comments demonstrate that the content generates conversation, which is precisely what TikTok wants to amplify.

- A comment is worth more than a like.
- Always respond to comments, especially in the first few hours after posting.
- Use comments as intelligence for future video ideas.

Strategy: End every video with a direct question. "What type of yoga do you practise?" guarantees comments.

---

### Signal 4 — Likes

Likes matter, but they are the least valuable of the four primary signals. Do not optimise for likes alone; focus on the signals above.

---

### Signal 5 — Follows from the Video

When someone watches your video and then follows your account, it is a strong signal that your content has a "want to see more" quality.

---

## 3. How Layered Distribution Works

When you upload a video, TikTok distributes it in stages:

```
Layer 1: ~200–500 users (small test audience)
         Is watch time and engagement strong?
Layer 2: ~5,000–10,000 users
         Is it still performing?
Layer 3: ~50,000–500,000 users
         Is it still performing?
Layer 4: Millions (viral)
```

If the video fails to pass the threshold at any layer, distribution stops. A video with 200 views is therefore not necessarily a failure — it simply did not pass the first test.

---

## 4. The Interest Algorithm — How Your Audience Finds You

TikTok categorises each video by topic and shows it to users who have previously engaged with similar content. The signals it uses for this include:

- On-screen text and subtitles
- What you say (audio is automatically transcribed)
- Hashtags
- Video description
- Music or sounds used

For Soulmigo: Talk about yoga, yoga socks, grip, practice, and mindfulness in your videos. TikTok detects this and surfaces your content to users interested in yoga.

---

## 5. The First 30 Minutes Are Critical

TikTok pays particular attention to engagement in the first few hours after posting. Best practices include:

1. Post during peak activity hours: 7–9am, 12–3pm, 7–11pm (UK time)
2. Respond to comments immediately — stay active at the moment of publication
3. Do not delete videos with low views — they can take off days later
4. Republish older content that performed well — TikTok can redistribute it

---

## 6. The Loop — Why Short Videos Perform Better

TikTok counts a view as complete when the video ends. If the video loops automatically and the user does not scroll away, the algorithm interprets this as extremely high retention — as though the user found it so compelling they watched it twice.

Tip: Design the end of your video to connect visually with the beginning. Users loop without realising it, and TikTok rewards that as very strong retention.

---

## 7. Consistency — Training the Algorithm

The algorithm learns from your account over time:

- A minimum of 3 to 5 videos per week for the first 8 weeks
- The more consistently you post on the same topic, the more clearly TikTok understands who you are and who to show you to
- Switching topics constantly confuses the algorithm

For Soulmigo: Stay focused on yoga, wellness, and yoga socks. Do not mix in unrelated lifestyle content.

---

## 8. Sounds and Trends

The algorithm favours videos that use trending sounds because those sounds already have established audiences searching for them.

- Use the Trending Sounds tab in the TikTok editor
- Pairing a trending sound with product content generates additional reach at no cost
- Original sounds can themselves become trending if the video performs well

## 11. How the Algorithm Categorises Your Account

Your first 5 to 10 videos are the most important in your TikTok history. They determine which niche the algorithm assigns you to and which audience it shows your content to.

### Signals Used for Categorisation

- Words spoken in the video (automatically transcribed)
- On-screen text and subtitles
- Video description
- Hashtags
- Sounds used
- Accounts you interact with on the platform

### Common Mistake for New Accounts

Posting a yoga video, then a food video, then a travel video. The algorithm cannot categorise you and shows your content to random audiences. The result is consistently low views across all posts.

For Soulmigo: Your first 10 videos must be 100% within the yoga and wellness space. Quality matters less at this stage than consistency of topic. The priority is training the algorithm to understand who you are.

## 12. The Interest Graph — How Your Ideal Audience Finds You

TikTok operates two parallel engines simultaneously.

### Engine 1 — Social Graph (who you follow)

Shows content from accounts the user already follows. This has a relatively low weight in the FYP.

### Engine 2 — Interest Graph (what you engage with)

Shows content based on past interactions. This carries the majority of weight in the FYP.

```
A person who:
- Watches yoga videos all the way through
- Follows wellness accounts
- Searches for "yoga socks"

TikTok will show them your video even if they have never heard of you.
```

Practical conclusion: You do not need followers to reach your ideal customer. You need your video to speak the same language your audience uses when consuming content.

## 13. Hidden Signals — What Most Creators Overlook

In addition to likes, comments, and shares, TikTok measures a number of less visible signals that carry significant weight:

| Hidden Signal | What It Means for the Algorithm |
|---|---|
| Replay | The video was compelling enough to watch twice — very positive signal |
| Save to Favourites | The user wants to return to it — indicates high-value content |
| Profile Visit | The video generated curiosity about your brand |
| Exit Time | Exiting at second 2 is a penalisation signal |
| Skip in First Second | The hook failed — strong negative signal |
| Pause Mid-Video | The user is processing information — positive signal |

Tip for saves: Create videos framed as "save this for when you practise" — tutorials, yoga sequences, routines. These are saved naturally and consistently.

## 14. Hashtags on TikTok — Different from Instagram

On Instagram, hashtags are the primary discovery mechanism. On TikTok, they function differently — they serve as an additional categorisation signal only.

### How to Use Them Correctly

Incorrect approach:

```
#yoga #yogasocks #fitness #wellness #tiktok #fyp #viral #foryou #uk
(too many, too generic, minimal impact)
```

Correct approach:

```
#yogasocks #yogauk #yogaforbeginners
(3 to 5 niche-specific hashtags)
```

### Practical Rules

- 2 to 3 niche-specific hashtags: #yogasocks, #yogauk
- 1 broader category hashtag: #yoga, #pilates
- Never use #fyp or #viral — these have no measurable effect and are widely considered a myth

### Important Note

The algorithm reads the content of your video directly, not just the hashtags. If you say "yoga socks" in the video, TikTok already knows — with or without the hashtag. Hashtags confirm the topic; they do not create discovery.

## 15. Re-Distribution — Old Videos That Come Back to Life

One of TikTok's most powerful and underappreciated features: the algorithm can redistribute old videos days or even weeks after they were originally posted.

### When This Happens

- A recent video performs well, and TikTok reviews your older content
- An influential account shares one of your older videos
- A sound you used in a past video subsequently becomes trending
- The algorithm runs periodic re-distribution cycles

### What This Means in Practice

- Never delete a video with low views — it may gain traction weeks later
- If a video underperforms, review it, but leave it published
- Save your best content as drafts to republish at a later date

## 16. Comment Strategy — The Most Controllable Signal

Comments are the signal you have the most direct ability to influence. Use these proven tactics:

### Tactic 1 — End Every Video with a Question

Ask something direct and easy to answer:

- "Do you practise yoga barefoot or with socks?"
- "How many times a week do you get on the mat?"
- "Do you prefer home practice or studio?"

### Tactic 2 — Reply with a Video

When a comment is interesting or frequently repeated, respond with a new video. TikTok notifies everyone who commented on the original, multiplying its reach significantly.

### Tactic 3 — Pin the First Comment

Post the first comment yourself and pin it. Frame it to invite conversation:
"Does anyone else hate slipping on the mat? Tell me below."

### Tactic 4 — The First Two Hours Are Critical

Respond to every comment within the first two hours of posting. The algorithm measures the speed of interaction as a quality indicator.

## 17. Anatomy of a High-Performing Video (0 to 30 Seconds)

```
0–2 seconds:   HOOK       — The pattern interrupt. This decides if they stay.
2–5 seconds:   PROMISE    — What they will see or learn if they keep watching.
5–22 seconds:  VALUE      — The actual content. Clear, fast, useful.
22–28 seconds: TWIST      — A surprise, unexpected insight, or reveal.
28–30 seconds: CTA        — A question, "follow for more", or a loop back to the start.
```

### Example for Soulmigo

```
HOOK (0–2s):    "Why I always slip on my mat... [pause]"
PROMISE (2–5s): "...and what changed everything"
VALUE (5–22s):  Demonstrating the anatomical grip in a real yoga pose
TWIST (22–28s): "The surprising part is that they're socks, not shoes"
CTA (28–30s):   "Do you practise with socks or barefoot?"
```

## 18. Penalisation Signals — What Kills a Video's Reach

Understanding what the algorithm penalises is as important as understanding what it rewards:

| Penalisation | Root Cause |
|---|---|
| Skip within seconds 1–2 | Weak or absent hook |
| Horizontal video | TikTok penalises content that is not natively vertical |
| Instagram/Reels watermark | Direct algorithmic penalisation |
| Poor audio quality | Prevents accurate automatic transcription |
| Text overlapping auto-subtitles | Poor user experience signal |
| External links in bio with low follower count | Platform-imposed reach restriction |
| Posting without engaging afterwards | No comment responses equals a negative signal |
