## How the TikTok Algorithm Works

Understanding the algorithm is not optional. Every creative decision — length, hook, format, posting time — should be informed by how TikTok distributes content.

### The distribution funnel

When you upload a video, TikTok does not show it to everyone. It tests it in stages:

1. **Micro-test (0–500 views):** The video is shown to a small group. TikTok evaluates watch time and immediate engagement.
2. **Expansion (500–10,000 views):** If the micro-test generates strong signals, distribution is widened.
3. **Broader reach (10,000–500,000 views):** The video enters the For You Page of users who have never seen your account.
4. **Viral (500,000+ views):** Sustained strong signals push the video to massive audiences. There is no ceiling.

A video can re-enter the funnel days or weeks after posting if engagement picks up again. TikTok does not penalise old content the way Instagram does.

### The signals the algorithm measures (ranked by weight)

| Signal | Weight | What it means |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Watch time / completion rate | Highest | Did the viewer watch the full video? Did they rewatch it? A replay counts as additional watch time. |
| Shares | Very high | A share is worth approximately 10x more than a like. |
| Comments | High | Written comments indicate the video provoked a reaction worth typing out. |
| Saves | High | Saving a video signals it has lasting value. One of the strongest signals for educational content. |
| Replays | High | If the viewer watches the video more than once, the algorithm interprets this as high-quality content. |
| Likes | Medium | A like is easy to give. It matters, but far less than shares, saves, or replays. |
| Profile visits after watching | Medium | If someone watches your video then visits your profile, TikTok interprets this as strong brand interest. |
| Follows after watching | Medium | A follow directly from a video view is a very strong positive signal. |
| Video information | Supporting | Hashtags, captions, text overlays, and audio help TikTok categorise your content and match it to the right audience. |

### What this means for you

- **Completion rate is king.** A 15-second video watched to the end beats a 60-second video abandoned at second 10. Start short (15–25 seconds).
- **Design for replays.** The loop is an enormously positive signal. Connect the visual ending of your video to the beginning to induce it naturally.
- **Encourage saves and shares, not just likes.** Videos that teach something get saved. Videos that surprise get shared. Videos that describe exactly how someone feels get sent to friends.
- **Hashtags matter for categorisation, not for reach.** Use specific hashtags so TikTok shows your video to the right micro-audience in stage 1. Broad hashtags like #fyp do nothing.
- **The first 2 hours are critical.** Reply to every comment immediately after posting. The algorithm measures the speed of interaction as a quality indicator.
- **Never delete videos with low views.** They can take off days or weeks later thanks to TikTok's redistribution cycles.

## Content Pillars

All Soulmigo content orbits around these 4 pillars. Every video should fit into one. This structure gives TikTok clarity about who you are and who to show your content to.

| Pillar | % of content | Purpose | Priority algorithm signal |
|--------|-------------|---------|--------------------------|

| Behind the scenes | 30% | Build emotional investment. Show the real process of building the brand. | Comments + watch time |
| Engagement (replies, stitches, duets) | 20% | Grow reach by riding existing audiences. | Profile visits + follows |
| Community and trends | 10% | Connect with ongoing conversations in the yoga/wellness UK niche. | Shares + comments |

**Rule for the first 10 videos:** The first 10 videos define which niche the algorithm assigns you to. They must be 100% within the yoga/wellness universe. Quality matters less than topic consistency at this stage.

## Brand Voice for Video

Before filming each video, align the content with these voice guidelines:

- **Like a friend who figured something out:** Speak as if you're explaining this to a fellow yogi friend, not reading a marketing script.
- **Specific over vague:**
  - Wrong: "I spent a lot of time designing this product"
  - Right: "I spent 6 months testing 12 different prototypes"
- **Emotion first:** Start with how you feel, not what you did. "I was so frustrated with slipping on the mat" connects more than "we created a non-slip sock".
- **Tone:** Warm, authentic, knowledgeable without being academic. Friendly without being informal.

## Strategy 1: Educational Content (40% of posts)

### Why this strategy

Your product solves a problem most people do not know they have. Educational content explains the invisible difference between a random grip and an anatomical grip. Additionally, educational videos get saved at a much higher rate than other formats. A saved video continues generating views for weeks.

### Format specifications

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Aspect ratio | 9:16 vertical (1080 x 1920 px) |
| Duration | 15–30 seconds (sweet spot for completion rate) |
| Frame rate | 30 fps |
| File format | MP4 |
| Text placement | Centre of screen, between 130px from top and 350px from bottom (safe zone — avoids UI overlaps) |
| Font | Bold sans-serif (Montserrat or similar). Minimum 48px. White text with dark shadow. |
| Text on screen | 5–10 words visible at a time. Change every 2–3 seconds. |

### Visual style and colour

| Role | Colour | Hex | When to use |
|------|--------|-----|-------------|
| Primary | Warm off-white / light beige | #F5F0EB | Backgrounds, surfaces, cards |
| Secondary | Forest green | #2D5F3E | Text headers, brand watermark, borders |
| Accent | Terracotta / warm clay | #C4704B | CTAs, key words, underlines |
| Main text | Charcoal | #2C2C2C | Text overlays on light backgrounds |
| Text on dark | Off-white | #F5F0EB | Text placed on dark backgrounds |

**The 60-30-10 rule for every video:**

- 60% dominant colour (the background: white card, beige mat, natural wood)
- 30% secondary colour (the product, text overlays)
- 10% accent colour (highlight one word, underline the CTA)

### The hook (first 2 seconds)

The hook determines whether someone watches or scrolls. It must create a curiosity gap or challenge an assumption. The hook should be spoken AND appear as text on screen simultaneously — many viewers watch muted.

| Formula | Example for Soulmigo |
|---------|---------------------|
| "Nobody tells you this about [X]" | "Nobody tells you this about yoga socks" |
| "Stop [common mistake]" | "Stop buying socks with random dots" |
| "The real reason [problem] happens" | "The real reason your grip socks slip" |
| "I spent [time] designing [X]" | "I spent 6 months and 12 prototypes designing this grip" |
| "[Common thing] is lying to you" | "Your grip socks are lying to you" |
| "This [detail] changes everything" | "This grip pattern changes everything" |
| "[Number] things that [outcome]" | "3 things to check before buying grip socks" |

### Structure of an educational video (15–25 seconds)

| Timestamp | What happens | On screen |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| 0–2 sec | HOOK — bold statement or question | Text: the hook phrase. Presenter speaking to camera or product close-up. |
| 2–5 sec | THE PROBLEM — why current solutions fail | Show a generic grip sock. Text: "Random dots. No anatomy. No logic." |
| 5–18 sec | THE SOLUTION — show the product and explain the difference | Close-up of the 3-zone grip. Point to each zone. Text overlay naming each zone. |
| 18–22 sec | THE PROOF — show it in action | Feet on a mat pressing down. Grip holding firm. |
| 22–25 sec | CTA | Question to drive comments: "Would you try these?" or "Socks or barefoot for yoga?" |

**Loop trick:** The CTA can visually return to the opening hook. If the video starts with a mat and ends with the same mat wearing Soulmigo socks, the loop is natural and the algorithm counts it as extra watch time.

### 10 specific video ideas

1. "Why your yoga socks slip (and what actually works)" — random dots vs anatomical grip
2. "3 things to check before buying grip socks" — material, grip pattern, sizing
3. "Organic cotton vs synthetic: what touches your skin matters" — feel test close-up
4. "The anatomy of your foot explains this grip" — map the 3 zones on a real foot
5. "I tested 5 grip socks. Only 1 passed." — side-by-side grip test on a mat
6. "What happens to grip socks after 10 washes" — durability comparison
7. "The size guide nobody gives you" — how to measure, which size to pick
8. "This is what 6 months of yoga sock research looks like" — design sketches, prototype iterations
9. "Pilates vs yoga: do you need different grip?" — explain the biomechanics
10. "The one thing your feet do that grip socks ignore" — toe splay, arch pressure

## Strategy 2: Behind the Scenes (30% of posts)

### Why this strategy

TikTok's algorithm penalises content that looks like advertising. It rewards content that feels native — raw, unpolished, human. Behind-the-scenes content is inherently native.

There is a second strategic reason: people develop emotional investment in products they watch being created. By September, your audience will have followed the journey for 4 months. They are no longer cold traffic — they are invested participants who want to see the product succeed.

### Format specifications

| Parameter | Difference from Strategy 1 |
|-----------|---------------------------|
| Duration | 20–45 seconds |
| Visual style | Deliberately unpolished. Phone camera. Natural lighting. No ring light. |
| Text overlays | Minimal. 1–2 lines maximum. Let the video speak. |
| Sound | Your voice narrating, or trending sound with text overlay. No stock music. |
| Editing | Simple cuts. No transitions. No effects. |

### Why "unpolished" outperforms "professional"

TikTok users scroll past polished ads. Overly produced videos trigger ad blindness. Native-looking content has 2x the engagement of produced ads. Creators who show their face get 1.5–2x more comments than faceless accounts.

This does not mean sloppy: clean background, good natural light, steady hand, clear audio.

### Structure of a BTS video (20–40 seconds)

| Timestamp | What happens |
|-----------|-------------|
| 0–2 sec | Context hook: "The samples just arrived from China" / "Day 47 of building a sock brand" |
| 2–30 sec | Show the process: open the package, hold the product, examine details, react naturally |
| 30–40 sec | Honest reflection or question: "The grip feels right but the colour is off. What do you think?" |

**Tip for saves:** Frame BTS videos as "save this to understand what quality yoga product development looks like". People save content they perceive as valuable to return to.

### 10 specific video ideas

1. "The first sample just arrived" — unboxing on camera, natural reaction
2. "Testing the grip on 3 different surfaces" — mat, wooden floor, tile
3. "I wash-tested these 5 times. Here is what happened." — before/after comparison
4. "Comparing 3 factory samples side by side" — lay them out, point out differences
5. "The packaging prototype is here" — show the mesh bag and belly band
6. "What it looks like to pack 100 pairs by hand" — time-lapse of the process
7. "My design sketches vs the final product" — show the evolution
8. "An honest look at what this costs to make" — component breakdown
9. "The factory sent the wrong colour. Now what?" — real problems, real solutions
10. "Day 1 vs Day 120 of building Soulmigo" — progress montage

### Filming checklist (before every BTS video)

- [ ] Natural light source (window or outdoor). No overhead fluorescent.
- [ ] Clean, uncluttered background. Neutral tones (white, beige, wood).
- [ ] Phone at eye level or slightly above. Never shoot looking up.
- [ ] Audio check: no background noise. Speak clearly, close to the mic.
- [ ] Product visible within first 3 seconds.
- [ ] Duration check: can this be said in under 30 seconds? If not, split into parts.

## Strategy 3: Engagement — Replies, Stitches, Duets (20% of posts)

### Why this strategy

This is the most underrated strategy for growing a new account. TikTok gives preferential treatment to content that interacts with other content on the platform:

- **Reply-to-comment videos** appear in the commenter's notifications AND in the feeds of people who saw the original video.
- **Stitch videos** get distributed to the original creator's audience in addition to yours.
- **Duet videos** work the same way.

For a new account with zero followers, this is the fastest path to visibility.

### Reply-to-comment videos

**When to use:** When someone leaves a comment that can be expanded into a full video response.

**What makes a good reply video:**

- The comment asks a genuine question: "How does the grip work?" → show a demonstration
- The comment is sceptical: "These look like regular socks" → show the difference up close
- The comment is a compliment you can expand on: "The packaging is so nice" → show how you designed it

**Seed your own replies:** In your first weeks, ask friends to leave specific comments you can reply to:

- "How is this different from normal grip socks?"
- "Do they work on wooden floors?"
- "What size should I get?"

### Stitch videos

**How to find target videos:**

1. Search TikTok: `yoga essentials`, `yoga mat slipping`, `pilates grip`, `yoga socks review`
2. Filter for videos with 10K+ views (proven the topic has interest)
3. Identify a 2–5 second clip that sets up a problem your product solves

**Structure:**

| Timestamp | Content |
|-----------|---------|
| 0–5 sec | The stitched clip from the original video (sets up the problem) |
| 5–7 sec | Your reaction: "Actually, there is a better option" |
| 7–20 sec | Show your product solving the problem |
| 20–25 sec | CTA or question |

**Examples:**

- Original: someone complaining socks slip in downward dog → Stitch + Soulmigo grip holding firm
- Original: "what is in my yoga bag" → Stitch + "missing one thing"
- Original: "yoga essentials for beginners" → Stitch + "nobody mentions the most important one"

### Duet videos

**When to use:** When the visual comparison between your reaction/product and the original adds value.

**Examples:**

- Original: someone doing yoga barefoot and slipping → You: same pose with Soulmigo, no slip
- Original: "rate my yoga setup" → You: reacting and adding socks to the setup
- Original: "yoga pet peeves" → You: nodding and showing the solution

## Strategy 4: Community and Trends (10% of posts)

### Why this pillar matters

The algorithm favours accounts that actively participate in ongoing conversations on the platform. This 10% allows connecting with trends in the yoga/wellness UK niche without losing brand coherence.

### How to execute

- **Niche trending sounds:** Use the "Trending sounds" tab in the editor. Combine trending sound + your product content.
- **Visual trends:** Adapt popular formats (POV, "things that just make sense", "tell me you're a yogi without telling me") to the product.
- **Filter rule:** Only use trends that genuinely fit the content and brand voice. Forced trends are detectable and penalising.

## Competitor Analysis — Reverse Engineering

Before scaling content creation, identify what already works in your niche. Do not guess; extract patterns from what already succeeds.

### Step 1 — Identify 10–15 niche creators

**Criteria:**

- Posting 3+ times per week about yoga, pilates, or wellness in the UK
- High engagement rate relative to follower count
- Audience overlaps with yours (women 25–45, UK)

**Where to find them:**

- Search TikTok: `yoga uk`, `yoga socks`, `pilates uk`, `yoga essentials`
- Filter by "Most liked" within the last 3 months
- Note accounts with 10K–500K followers (more attainable than mega-accounts)

### Step 2 — Collect the top 20 highest-engagement videos per account

For each high-performing video, note:

- The hook (first 2–3 words)
- The format (educational, BTS, reaction, humour, tutorial)
- The duration
- The CTA used
- Why you think it worked

### Step 3 — Extract patterns

Answer these questions with your data:

- What hook types appear most in the top 10% of videos?
- What duration does the average high-performing video have?
- Which formats repeat most? Tutorials? Reactions? BTS?
- Which CTAs generate the most comments?

### Step 4 — Apply patterns with Soulmigo's voice

Take the patterns that work in the niche and apply them with Soulmigo's brand voice (warm, expert, authentic). Do not copy content — replicate the structure and engagement mechanism.

## Daily Engagement Routine (30 minutes)

This routine is not optional. TikTok rewards accounts that are active on the platform, not just accounts that post.

| Time | Activity | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| 5 min | Reply to every comment on your videos | Signals to the algorithm that your content generates active conversation. |
| 15 min | Leave value-adding comments on 5–10 yoga/pilates accounts in the UK | Exposes your profile to their audience. Do not promote — add genuine value. Example: "That transition was so smooth. How long did it take you to nail it?" |
| 5 min | Find 1 video to stitch or duet | Build your engagement content queue for the next day. Save it to favourites. |
| 5 min | Browse #yogauk #yogasocks #pilatesuk and interact | Trains TikTok to correctly categorise your account. |

**Critical:** Complete this routine AFTER posting your video, within 30 minutes of posting. The immediate post-publication activity signals to TikTok that your account is active, which can boost the initial distribution of the video you just posted.

## Technical Production Guide

### Equipment needed (total cost: £0)

| Item | What to use |
|------|-------------|
| Camera | Your phone. Rear camera, not selfie camera. |
| Tripod | Stack of books + lean phone against something. Or a £10 phone tripod from Amazon. |
| Lighting | Natural window light. Position yourself facing the window. Never have the window behind you. |
| Microphone | Your phone's built-in mic is sufficient if the room is quiet. Speak clearly, close to the phone. |
| Editing | TikTok's built-in editor or CapCut (free and more powerful). |
| Graphics | Canva (free plan) for infographics, size guides, comparison images. |

### Filming setup

```
[Window / natural light]
        |
        v
    [Subject / product]
        |
    [Phone camera, 60–90 cm away]
```

- Shoot against a clean background: white wall, wooden table, or yoga mat on the floor.
- Remove clutter from the frame. Every object in frame should be intentional.
- For product shots: use a white card (£1 from any art shop) as a background.
- For talking-head shots: frame from chest up. Eyes in the upper third of the screen.
- For demonstration shots: frame the feet/product filling 60–70% of the screen.

### Audio

- Speak in a conversational tone — as if explaining to a friend, not a presentation voice.
- If using trending sounds: add text overlays for every key point.
- Always add captions. TikTok has an auto-caption feature. Review and correct errors before posting. 40–50% of TikTok viewing is muted.

### Text overlay rules

- Maximum 2 lines of text visible at once
- Minimum font size: 48px
- Bold sans-serif fonts (Montserrat, Inter, or TikTok's built-in "Classic")
- White text with dark shadow outperforms coloured text on most backgrounds
- Centre text horizontally
- Safe zone: between 130px from the top and 350px from the bottom
- Change text every 2–3 seconds

## Hashtag Strategy

Hashtags serve one purpose: telling TikTok which micro-audience to show your video to in stage 1 of distribution. They do not boost reach on their own. The algorithm reads video content directly — if you say "yoga socks" in the video, TikTok already knows with or without the hashtag.

### Structure per post

Use 3–5 hashtags per video. More than 5 dilutes the signal.

| Category | Hashtags | Purpose |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Niche (2–3) | #yogasocks #gripsocks #pilatesequipment | Categorise your content precisely |
| Location (1) | #yogauk or #fitnessuk | Target UK viewers |
| Topic (1–2) | #yogaessentials #yogabeginner #yogagear | Reach people browsing that topic |

### Hashtags to avoid

- #fyp #foryou #foryoupage — too broad, TikTok ignores them for categorisation
- #viral #trending — no targeting value
- Hashtags with 100B+ views — your video drowns in the volume

### Soulmigo-specific hashtags

Primary: `#yogasocks` `#gripsocks` `#yogauk` `#pilatesuk`  
Secondary: `#yogaessentials` `#yogagear` `#yogabeginner` `#yogareview`  
Contextual: `#yogamat` `#hotyoga` `#yogainstructor` `#yogalife` `#smallbusinessuk`

## Measuring Results

### Weekly metrics

| Metric | Where to find it | Target for a new account |
|--------|-----------------|--------------------------|
| Average watch time % | TikTok Analytics → Content | Above 50%. Ideal: 80%+ |
| Completion rate | TikTok Analytics → Content | Above 30% for videos under 30 sec |
| Shares | TikTok Analytics → Content | Any shares are a strong signal |
| Saves | TikTok Analytics → Content | Above 1% of views |
| Replays | TikTok Analytics → Content | Any replay is positive; aim for >5% |
| Profile visits | TikTok Analytics → Overview | Increasing week over week |
| Follower growth | TikTok Analytics → Overview | 50–100/week is realistic in the first months |
| Comments | Under each video | Responding to all matters more than the count |

### What to do with the data

- **If a video gets above-average views:** Make a follow-up. Same topic, different angle.
- **If completion rate is below 20%:** Your hook is failing. Test a different opening line.
- **If views are fine but no profile visits:** Your CTA is missing or weak. Add "follow for more" or "link in bio".
- **If one format outperforms others:** Do more of that format. The algorithm has told you what your audience wants.
- **If watch time is high but saves are low:** The content entertains but does not educate. Increase informational value.

## Anatomy of a High-Performing Video (0–30 seconds)

```
0–2 sec:   HOOK      — The pattern interrupt. Whether they stay is decided here.
2–5 sec:   PROMISE   — What they will see or learn if they keep watching.
5–22 sec:  VALUE     — The actual content. Clear, fast, useful.
22–28 sec: TWIST     — A surprise, unexpected insight, or result.
28–30 sec: CTA       — A question, "follow for more", or a visual loop back to the start.
```

**Example for Soulmigo:**

```
HOOK (0–2s):    "Why I keep slipping on my mat... [pause]"
PROMISE (2–5s): "...and what changed everything"
VALUE (5–22s):  Show the anatomical grip working in a real yoga pose
TWIST (22–28s): "The surprising part is they're socks, not shoes"
CTA (28–30s):   "Do you practise yoga with socks or barefoot?"
```

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why it hurts | What to do instead |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Posting horizontal video | Appears with black bars. Algorithm deprioritises it. Looks amateur. | Always shoot 9:16 vertical. |
| Hard selling in every video | TikTok users swipe away from ads. The algorithm detects promotional language. | Provide value first. Sell indirectly through demonstrations. |
| Ignoring comments | The algorithm measures whether you engage. Ignored comments signal an inactive account. | Reply to every comment within 2 hours. |
| Inconsistent posting | The algorithm learns your posting rhythm. A 2-week gap penalises your next video. | Post minimum 3x/week. Never more than 3 days without posting. |
| Using trending sounds that do not fit | Forced trends feel inauthentic. The sound's audience may not overlap with yours. | Only use trending sounds when they genuinely fit the content. |
| Too much text on screen | Cluttered visuals cause scrolling. | Maximum 2 lines at a time. Change every 2–3 seconds. |
| Copying other brands exactly | TikTok deprioritises recycled concepts. | Use proven hook formulas applied to your unique angle: anatomical grip, organic cotton, UK. |
| Not adding captions | 40–50% of TikTok viewing is muted. No captions = losing half your audience. | Always use TikTok's auto-captions. Review and correct errors. |
| Deleting videos with low views | Videos can take off weeks later thanks to redistribution cycles. | Never delete. Always leave published. |
| Not optimising for the replay loop | The loop is one of the strongest algorithm signals. | Connect the visual end of the video to the beginning. |
