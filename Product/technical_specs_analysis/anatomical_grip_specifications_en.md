Manufacturing Specifications (Blueprint Data)

Anatomical Flow Grip

### 1. Relief & Profile (Thickness Profile)

* **General Thickness:** **0.60mm - 0.80mm** (Tolerance: ±0.05mm)
* **Material:** High-Density Silicone.
* **Technical Rationale:**
  * **< 0.6mm (Risk):** Premature wear and insufficient traction on damp surfaces.
  * **> 0.8mm (Risk):** Loss of proprioception (ground feel), preventing proper balance feedback in standing asanas.
  * **Result:** The 0.6-0.8mm range offers the optimal biomechanical balance.

### 2. Trace Dimensions (Line Dimensions)

* **Metatarsal Zone (Push-off):**
  * *Line Width:* **3.5mm**
  * *Spacing (Kerning):* **2.0mm**
  * *Reason:* Maximizes contact area under the ball of the foot to enhance push-off and braking power.
* **Plantar Arch Zone (Support):**
  * *Line Width:* **1.8mm**
  * *Spacing:* **3.0mm**
  * *Reason:* Thinner, more widely spaced lines to allow full arch flexibility without structural stiffness.
* **Calcaneal Zone (Heel):**
  * *Width:* **3.0mm** ("U" Shape Design)
  * *Reason:* Lateral stabilization of the rearfoot upon impact.

## 🧠 Biomechanical Rationale: "Form follows Function"

This design is not merely aesthetic; it responds to the functional anatomy of the foot in motion:

1. **Transverse Waves (Metatarsals):**
    * **Function:** Articulation.
    * **Explanation:** The lines follow the phalangeal flexion axes. When performing a *Relevé* (tiptoes), the grip flexes *with* the foot, preventing the silicone from peeling or creating resistance.

2. **Longitudinal Lines (Arch):**
    * **Function:** Dynamic Tension.
    * **Explanation:** Act as external tensors hugging the midfoot, preventing sock rotation during twists.

3. **Horseshoe Cup (Heel):**
    * **Function:** Stability.
    * **Explanation:** "Cradles" the calcaneus bone, centering the load and preventing lateral displacement within the sock.
