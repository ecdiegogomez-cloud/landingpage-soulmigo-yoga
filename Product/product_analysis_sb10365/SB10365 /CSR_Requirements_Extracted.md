# Wolverine & Sweaty Betty Supply Chain Compliance Standards

## 1. Required Certifications & Standards

* **SA8000 Standard:** Social compliance audits must use the SA8000 standard for human rights.
* **Bureau Veritas Certification:** Internal auditors must be certified by Bureau Veritas.
* **Third-Party Audits:** External audit reports are accepted if they satisfy Sweaty Betty’s minimum requirements (for Sweaty Betty Suppliers).

## 2. Code of Conduct Parameters

### Core Requirements

* **Compliance:** Mandatory adherence to *Wolverine Production Code of Conduct* AND *Sweaty Betty Supply Chain Code of Conduct*.
* **Human Rights:** Dignity, respect, and fair treatment.
* **Wages:** Must pay living wages or international equivalent.
* **Employment:** Must be freely chosen (no forced labor).
* **Working Hours:** Must not be excessive.
* **Treatment:** Prohibition of harsh or inhumane treatment.

### Zero-Tolerance Policies

* Modern Slavery & Human Trafficking
* Child Labor
* Forced or Involuntary Labor
* Retaliation, Harassment, Bullying

## 3. Due Diligence & Risk Management

* **Onboarding:** All new partners are risk-assessed prior to onboarding.
* **Conflict Zones:** Enhanced human rights due diligence required for suppliers operating in or sourcing from conflict-affected areas.
* **Grievance Mechanisms:** Must be in place and assessed.

## 4. Auditing Protocol

* **Frequency:**
  * **Core Production Partners:** At least Annually.
  * **Sweaty Betty Suppliers:** Must have up-to-date third-party audits.
* **Method:** Combination of Internal Auditors (China/Vietnam) and External Audit Firms.
* **Unannounced Inspections:** Wolverine reserves the right to conduct these at any time.
* **Remediation:** Mandatory Corrective Action Plans (CAP) for material issues within agreed timeframes.
* **Termination:** Failure to address severe issues leads to termination of relationship.
